Day Three

You've collected fragments of stories from the trees. You've milked the cows and begun to churn the butter and outside the fruit is ripening in the orchard. You hear a knock at the door.

Do you answer the door? Or do you ignore it?

You open the door and forbidden words force their way in, blowing through your hair and rippling through your clothes, swirling round your house. They shake the wooden walls and the tiled roof. They swarm at your chimney stack until it crashes to the ground and the cat screeches and you beg or you fight it doesn't matter, because the roof is opening out now, peeling back like skin on fruit, opening up to the grey sky above.

The words are gone, and you can't remember a single one of them.
